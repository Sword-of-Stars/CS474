\title{\textbf{Chomsky Normal Form Converter}}
\author{Scooter Computer}

The following is a conversion of a context-free grammar (CFG) into Chomsky Normal Form (CNF). \\

This conversion has five steps:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Ensure the start variable is not on the RHS
\item Eliminate $\epsilon$-rules by first finding nullable variables
\item Eliminate unit rules
\item RHS is either one terminal or just variables
\item Ensure RHS has at most two variables
\end{enumerate}
Right-hand side will be abbreviated to RHS and left-hand side will be abbreviated to LHS.

\section*{Original Grammar}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to AaB  | b  | S \\
    A &\to S  | \varepsilon  | AB \\
    B &\to bbb  | ASA \\
    \end{align*}

\section{Ensure the start variable is not on the RHS}

The grammar contains productions with \(S\) on the RHS. To avoid circularity and ensure CNF prerequisites, we add a fresh start variable:
\[
S_0 \to S
\]
This guarantees that the start variable does not appear on any RHS.


\section{Eliminate \(\varepsilon\)-rules}

\subsection*{Step 1: Identify Nullable Variables}
A variable is \emph{nullable} if it can derive \(\varepsilon\).
We identify nullable variables iteratively:
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item \textbfDirect: Found
  \(\{A\}\). Variables that have an explicit ε-production.
  
  \item \textbfIndirect: Found
  \(\{B, S\}\). Variables that derive strings of only nullable variables.
  
  \item \textbfIndirect: Found
  \(\{S0\}\). Variables that derive strings of only nullable variables.
  
\end{itemize}
Thus,
\[
V_{\varepsilon}=\{A, B, S, S0\}.
\]

\subsection*{Step 2: Add Productions Erasing Nullable Variables}
For each \(X\in V_{\varepsilon}\), we add rules where \(X\) is removed from contexts in which it appears (keeping language equivalence).
We record all additions step-by-step:


\paragraph{Processing nullable variable \(\mathbf{ A }\).}
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item For \( S \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{AaB, b, S\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{AaB, aB\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( A \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{S, \varepsilon, AB\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{AB, B\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( B \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{bbb, ASA\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{AS, ASA, S, SA\}
    \]
  
\end{itemize}
After processing \( A \), snapshot:
\begin{align*}
    S &\to AaB  | S  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to AB  | B  | S  | \varepsilon \\
    B &\to AS  | ASA  | S  | SA  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to S \\
    \end{align*}

\paragraph{Processing nullable variable \(\mathbf{ B }\).}
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item For \( S \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{AaB, S, aB, b\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{Aa, AaB, a, aB\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( A \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{AB, B, S, \varepsilon\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{\varepsilon, A, AB, B\}
    \]
  
\end{itemize}
After processing \( B \), snapshot:
\begin{align*}
    S &\to Aa  | AaB  | S  | a  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AB  | B  | S  | \varepsilon \\
    B &\to AS  | ASA  | S  | SA  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to S \\
    \end{align*}

\paragraph{Processing nullable variable \(\mathbf{ S }\).}
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item For \( S \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{Aa, AaB, S, a, aB, b\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{\varepsilon, S\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( A \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{, A, AB, B, S, \varepsilon\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{\varepsilon, S\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( B \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{AS, ASA, S, SA, bbb\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{\varepsilon, A, AA, AS, ASA, S, SA\}
    \]
  
  \item For \( S0 \):
    \[
      \text{Original: } \{S\}
    \]
    \[
      \text{Added: } \{\varepsilon, S\}
    \]
  
\end{itemize}
After processing \( S \), snapshot:
\begin{align*}
    S &\to \varepsilon  | Aa  | AaB  | S  | a  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AB  | B  | S  | \varepsilon \\
    B &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AA  | AS  | ASA  | S  | SA  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to \varepsilon  | S \\
    \end{align*}


\subsection*{Step 3: Remove Explicit \(\varepsilon\)-Productions}
We now delete all explicit \(\varepsilon\)-rules:

Removed from \(\{A\}\).


\paragraph{Grammar after \(\varepsilon\)-elimination (post-additions, pre-removal).}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to \varepsilon  | Aa  | AaB  | S  | a  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AB  | B  | S  | \varepsilon \\
    B &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AA  | AS  | ASA  | S  | SA  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to \varepsilon  | S \\
    \end{align*}

\paragraph{Grammar after removing explicit \(\varepsilon\)-rules.}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to \varepsilon  | Aa  | AaB  | S  | a  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AB  | B  | S \\
    B &\to \varepsilon  | A  | AA  | AS  | ASA  | S  | SA  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to \varepsilon  | S \\
    \end{align*}

\section{Eliminate unit rules}

A \emph{unit rule} has the form \(A \to B\), where \(A,B\) are variables. We substitute along reachability so that unit rules become unnecessary.

\subsection*{Step 1: Visualize unit-rule reachability}
\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.55\linewidth]{ phase2/chomsky_converter/out\unit_graph.png }
    \caption{Unit-rule reachability graph (\(A\to B\) edges).}
    \label{fig:unit_graph}
\end{figure}

\subsection*{Step 2: Substitute productions along unit paths}
For each variable \(A\), we add all non-unit productions from variables reachable from \(A\):
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item For \( A \), reachable: \(\{A, B, S\}\).
        Added: \(\{, AA, AB, AS, ASA, Aa, AaB, SA, a, aB, b, bbb\}\).
  
  \item For \( B \), reachable: \(\{A, B, S\}\).
        Added: \(\{, AA, AB, AS, ASA, Aa, AaB, SA, a, aB, b, bbb\}\).
  
  \item For \( S \), reachable: \(\{S\}\).
        Added: \(\{, Aa, AaB, a, aB, b\}\).
  
  \item For \( S0 \), reachable: \(\{S\}\).
        Added: \(\{, Aa, AaB, a, aB, b\}\).
  
</itemize}

\subsection*{Step 3: Remove unit rules}

Removed unit rules:
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item \(S	o S\)
  
  \item \(A	o A\)
  
  \item \(A	o B\)
  
  \item \(A	o S\)
  
  \item \(B	o A\)
  
  \item \(B	o S\)
  
  \item \(S0	o S\)
  
\end{itemize}


\paragraph{Grammar after unit-rule elimination.}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to   | Aa  | AaB  | a  | aB  | b \\
    A &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | ASA  | Aa  | AaB  | SA  | a  | aB  | b  | bbb \\
    B &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | ASA  | Aa  | AaB  | SA  | a  | aB  | b  | bbb \\
    S0 &\to   | Aa  | AaB  | a  | aB  | b \\
    \end{align*}

\section{Ensure RHS is either one terminal or just variables}
If a rule mixes terminals with variables on the RHS (length \(>1\) and contains a terminal), we replace each terminal \(a\) with a variable \(U_a\) (shown as \([Ua]\) internally), adding \(U_a \to a\).

\subsection*{Replacements performed}

\begin{itemize}
  
  \item For \( S \):
    
    \[
      S \to Aa
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S \to A[Ua]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      S \to AaB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S \to A[Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      S \to aB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S \to [Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
  
  \item For \( A \):
    
    \[
      A \to Aa
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      A \to A[Ua]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      A \to AaB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      A \to A[Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      A \to aB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      A \to [Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      A \to bbb
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      A \to [Ub][Ub][Ub]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }b→[Ub], b→[Ub], b→[Ub]\text{)}
    \]
    
  
  \item For \( B \):
    
    \[
      B \to Aa
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      B \to A[Ua]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      B \to AaB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      B \to A[Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      B \to aB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      B \to [Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      B \to bbb
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      B \to [Ub][Ub][Ub]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }b→[Ub], b→[Ub], b→[Ub]\text{)}
    \]
    
  
  \item For \( S0 \):
    
    \[
      S0 \to Aa
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S0 \to A[Ua]
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      S0 \to AaB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S0 \to A[Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
    \[
      S0 \to aB
      \quad\Rightarrow\quad
      S0 \to [Ua]B
      \quad\text{(replaced: }a→[Ua]\text{)}
    \]
    
  
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{New terminal variables created}
\begin{itemize}
  
  \item \( U_a \to a \)
  
  \item \( U_b \to b \)
  
\end{itemize}


\paragraph{Grammar after terminal replacements.}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to   | a  | b  | AU_a  | AU_aB  | U_aB \\
    A &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | ASA  | SA  | a  | b  | AU_a  | AU_aB  | U_aB  | U_bU_bU_b \\
    B &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | ASA  | SA  | a  | b  | AU_a  | AU_aB  | U_aB  | U_bU_bU_b \\
    S0 &\to   | a  | b  | AU_a  | AU_aB  | U_aB \\
    [Ua] &\to a \\
    [Ub] &\to b \\
    \end{align*}

\section{Break up long RHS rules}
If a production has more than two variables on the RHS (by tokens), we systematically break it into binary rules with fresh variables, reusing existing ones when possible.


\subsection*{Binary factoring steps}

\paragraph{For \( S \)}

\[
\text{Original: } S \to A[Ua]B
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( A[Ua] \to C (new) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } S \to CB
\]


\paragraph{For \( A \)}

\[
\text{Original: } A \to ASA
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( AS \to D (new) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } A \to DA
\]

\[
\text{Original: } A \to A[Ua]B
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( A[Ua] \to C (reused) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } A \to CB
\]

\[
\text{Original: } A \to [Ub][Ub][Ub]
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( [Ub][Ub] \to E (new) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } A \to E[Ub]
\]


\paragraph{For \( B \)}

\[
\text{Original: } B \to ASA
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( AS \to D (reused) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } B \to DA
\]

\[
\text{Original: } B \to A[Ua]B
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( A[Ua] \to C (reused) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } B \to CB
\]

\[
\text{Original: } B \to [Ub][Ub][Ub]
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( [Ub][Ub] \to E (reused) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } B \to E[Ub]
\]


\paragraph{For \( S0 \)}

\[
\text{Original: } S0 \to A[Ua]B
\]
\[
\text{Sequence: }\big( A[Ua] \to C (reused) \big)\]
\[
\text{Final: } S0 \to CB
\]



\subsection*{New variables created}

\begin{itemize}
  
  \item \( C \to A[Ua] \)
  
  \item \( D \to AS \)
  
  \item \( E \to [Ub][Ub] \)
  
\end{itemize}



\section*{Final Grammar (CNF)}
\begin{align*}
    S &\to   | a  | b  | AU_a  | U_aB  | CB \\
    A &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | SA  | a  | b  | AU_a  | U_aB  | DA  | CB  | EU_b \\
    B &\to   | AA  | AB  | AS  | SA  | a  | b  | AU_a  | U_aB  | DA  | CB  | EU_b \\
    S0 &\to   | a  | b  | AU_a  | U_aB  | CB \\
    [Ua] &\to a \\
    [Ub] &\to b \\
    C &\to AU_a \\
    D &\to AS \\
    E &\to U_bU_b \\
    \end{align*}

\noindent All productions now have either a single terminal on the RHS or exactly two variables on the RHS; thus the grammar is in Chomsky Normal Form.
